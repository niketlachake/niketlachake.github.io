Linear Vibration Absorbers: Types, Design Fundamentals, and Location in the Vehicle

  • Post On17.03.2023
  • ByNiket Lachake

Introduction:

Vibration is a natural phenomenon that occurs when an object is subjected to an external force. In the case of vehicles, vibration can be caused by a variety of factors, such as road irregularities, engine and drivetrain components, and wind. Vibration can have a number of negative effects on vehicles, including:

  • Reduced driver comfort
  • Increased fatigue
  • Increased wear and tear on components
  • Increased noise levels

There are two main types of linear vibration absorbers:

  1. Tuned mass dampers (TMDs)
  2. Tuned inerter dampers (TIDs)

TMDs are the most common type of linear vibration absorber. They consist of a mass, a spring, and a damper. The mass is attached to the vehicle through a spring, and the damper is connected between the mass and the vehicle body. The TMD is tuned to the resonant frequency of the vehicle, and when the vehicle vibrates, the TMD absorbs some of the energy and reduces the vibration amplitude.

TIDs are a newer type of linear vibration absorber. They consist of an inerter, a spring, and a damper. The inerter is a two-terminal device that produces a force proportional to the relative acceleration between its terminals. The TID is tuned to the resonant frequency of the vehicle, and when the vehicle vibrates, the TID absorbs some of the energy and reduces the vibration amplitude.

Design Fundamentals of Linear Vibration Absorbers

The design of a linear vibration absorber is based on the following principles:

  1. The resonant frequency of the absorber must be matched to the resonant frequency of the vehicle.
  2. The absorber must have enough mass to absorb enough energy to reduce the vibration amplitude to an acceptable level.
  3. The absorber must be stiff enough to prevent it from becoming unstable.
  4. The absorber must be damped to reduce the vibration amplitude.

Location of Linear Vibration Absorbers in the Vehicle

Linear vibration absorbers can be located in a variety of locations in the vehicle, depending on the application. Some common locations include:

  • The engine mounts
  • The suspension system
  • The drivetrain components
  • The body of the vehicle

The location of the absorber is important because it affects the effectiveness of the absorber. The absorber should be located as close as possible to the source of the vibration.

Conclusion

Linear vibration absorbers are a simple and effective way to reduce vibration levels in vehicles. They can improve driver comfort, reduce fatigue, and extend the life of components. When designing a linear vibration absorber, it is important to consider the resonant frequency of the vehicle, the mass of the absorber, the stiffness of the spring, and the damping coefficient. The absorber should also be located as close as possible to the source of the vibration.
